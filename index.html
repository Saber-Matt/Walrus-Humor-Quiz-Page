What Even Are Walruses

These blubbery behemoths come in two varies: Tusk-obvious and Tusk-elusive (shown above). Male Walruses sport an impressive weapon of dental destruction,
much like the African Elephant. You know, teeth, although the female walrus' also have tusks, they are much smaller than their male counterpart's.
Baby Walrus also have tusks which "erupt" during the calf's first summer or fall.

Who gets down with the Walri?

This majestic crusader has a long list of enemies with which to contend, stemming from a dark and sorted past.
The details of which I'm not at liberty to divulge without first obtaining adequate proof of your intentions.

Suffice to say walruses prefer to gather into groups of their own, where the only treachery comes from within. A group of walruses is called a herd.
Hundreds gather to sunbathe on the ice. During mating season, walruses amass by the thousands. Usually separated by gender, each with their own herds;
like the awkward beginning of a school dance.
